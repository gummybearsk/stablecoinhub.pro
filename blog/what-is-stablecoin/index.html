What Is a Stablecoin? Types, Examples, and How They Work

Quick Answer

A stablecoin is a type of cryptocurrency designed to maintain a stable value by pegging it to a reserve asset like the US dollar, gold, or other cryptocurrencies. Unlike volatile cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin or Ethereum, stablecoins aim to combine the benefits of digital currencies‚Äîlike instant transactions and programmability‚Äîwith the price stability of traditional assets.

Why Stablecoins Matter

Stablecoins solve one of cryptocurrency's biggest challenges: volatility. While Bitcoin can fluctuate 10-20% in a single day, stablecoins maintain their value, making them practical for:

  • Everyday transactions: Buy coffee without worrying about price changes
  • Store of value: Hold digital dollars without bank accounts
  • Trading pairs: Move between cryptocurrencies without converting to fiat
  • DeFi applications: Earn yield, borrow, and lend with predictable values
  • Cross-border payments: Send money internationally in seconds, not days

Types of Stablecoins

1. Fiat-Backed Stablecoins

These stablecoins are backed 1:1 by traditional currencies held in reserve.

  • USDT (Tether): Largest by market cap, $120 billion
  • USDC (USD Coin): Most regulated, $25 billion
  • BUSD (Binance USD): Exchange-backed

Pros: Simple, stable, widely accepted

Cons: Requires trust in issuer, regulatory risk

2. Crypto-Backed Stablecoins

Backed by cryptocurrency collateral, usually over-collateralized.

  • DAI: Backed by ETH and other crypto (150%+ collateral)
  • sUSD (Synthetix USD): Synthetic stablecoin

Pros: Decentralized, transparent, no single point of failure

Cons: Complex, capital inefficient, liquidation risk

3. Algorithmic Stablecoins

Use algorithms and smart contracts to maintain price stability.

  • FRAX: Partially algorithmic, partially backed
  • UST (Terra USD): Failed in 2022, lost $60 billion

Pros: Capital efficient, decentralized

Cons: Experimental, high risk, prone to death spirals

How Stablecoins Work

The mechanism depends on the type of stablecoin:

  1. Fiat-backed: For every stablecoin issued, the company holds $1 in a bank account. When you redeem 1 USDC, Circle burns the token and sends you $1.
  2. Crypto-backed: Users lock up $150 worth of ETH to mint $100 of DAI. If ETH price falls, the system automatically sells collateral to maintain backing.
  3. Algorithmic: Smart contracts automatically mint or burn tokens based on demand. If price > $1, mint more. If price < $1, burn supply.

Key Takeaways

  • Stablecoins maintain stable value by pegging to assets like the US dollar
  • Three main types: fiat-backed (USDC), crypto-backed (DAI), and algorithmic (FRAX)
  • Used for payments, trading, DeFi, and as a store of value
  • USDT dominates with $120B market cap but USDC leads in compliance
  • Choose based on your needs: trading (USDT), compliance (USDC), or DeFi (DAI)
